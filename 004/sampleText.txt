Pen we output text in C++, we don’t always want all of the information to appear on one line. This can result in output that’s tough to read. The users of your program will have a tough time finding specific points in a large block of information, also known as the dreaded “wall of text”. A chunk of text, also known as a “string” to C++ developers, is a sequential series of characters terminated by the special end-of-string character, written as ‘\0’. There are many other such special characters, such as tab `\t` and newline `\n` (the topic of this blog post).
